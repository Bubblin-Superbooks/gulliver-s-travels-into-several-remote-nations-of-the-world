The author returns to Maldonada.  Sails to the kingdom of Luggnagg.  The author confined.  He is sent for to court.  The manner of his admittance.  The king’s great lenity to his subjects.

The day of our departure being come, I took leave of his highness, the Governor of Glubbdubdrib, and returned with my two companions to Maldonada, where, after a fortnight’s waiting, a ship was ready to sail for Luggnagg.  The two gentlemen, and some others, were so generous and kind as to furnish me with provisions, and see me on board.  I was a month in this voyage.  We had one violent storm, and were under a necessity of steering westward to get into the trade wind, which holds for above sixty leagues.  On the 21st of April, 1708, we sailed into the river of Clumegnig, which is a seaport town, at the south-east point of Luggnagg.
