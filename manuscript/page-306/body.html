One day, a young gentleman, who was nephew to my nurse’s governess, came and pressed them both to see an execution.  It was of a man, who had murdered one of that gentleman’s intimate acquaintance.  Glumdalclitch was prevailed on to be of the company, very much against her inclination, for she was naturally tender-hearted: and, as for myself, although I abhorred such kind of spectacles, yet my curiosity tempted me to see something that I thought must be extraordinary.  The malefactor was fixed in a chair upon a scaffold erected for that purpose, and his head cut off at one blow, with a sword of about forty feet long.  The veins and arteries spouted up such a prodigious quantity of blood, and so high in the air, that the great jet d’
